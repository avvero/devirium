From [[Crucial Conversations]].

Unfortunately, as we grow older, most of us don't realize that this desire to win is continually driving us away from healthy dialogue. We start out with the goal of resolving a problem, but as soon as someone raises the red flag of inaccuracy or challenges our correctness, we switch purposes in a heartbeat ([[Focus on that you really want]]). First we correct the facts. We quibble over details and point out flaws in the other person's arguments.
